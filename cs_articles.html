Computer Science & Algorithms

Why write on these topics?

Depth and Breadth.

How deep is my understanding? It's a question I find myself constantly asking. I've heard it said that you don't truly understand something unless you can explain it to a seven-year-old. If that is literally true, I clearly have a long way to go! In reality, it points to an important truth - that simply parroting content is vastly different from having the depth of knowledge that allows you to approach and explore a topic from more than one angle. These articles are my learning process in disguise. Through them, I hope to deepen my own understanding.

How broad is my knowledge? Computer science and technology are incredibly expansive topics. For me, it means I can always look forward to knowing more tomorrow than I do today. Not only are these articles a learning process, they are also a catalog of the knowledge I've sought to grasp in some meaningful way. I hope you enjoy reading them as much as I enjoy writing them.

Big O Notation (Here We "O" Again)

What better way to kick off a CS & Algorithm articles section than with a review of Big-O Notation? It is as well-covered of a topic as you can get, yet it remains a bit of a mystery to many. In this article I review the core concepts of Big-O as well as its siblings, Big-Theta and Big-Omega, in as little time and complexity I can!
